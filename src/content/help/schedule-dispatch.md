The Schedule page (`/schedule-dispatch`) is where the office puts people on jobs. Only devs, masters, assistants, and superintendents can open it — and if you can see it, you can edit it.

## The views

The hub has three view tabs: **People**, **Jobs**, and **Day**. The People view is the workhorse — a weekly grid with people down the left and days across the top. Use the **Search Person or Job** box to jump around, and hide weekend columns when you don't need them. An **Expected manpower** readout totals who's scheduled.

## Assigning someone to a job

1. Click the add control in a person's cell for the day ("Add job to schedule for this person and day").
2. In the **Add job to schedule** modal, search by HCP number or job name, or press **Create new job**.
3. Pick the job — a schedule block appears in the cell. Jobs the person already clocked into today show a "Clocked today" badge.

Each block can carry a note: click the pencil ("Edit block note") to open the **Schedule block note** modal. Notes are what the tech sees about the assignment, so use them for gate codes, scope reminders, and arrival instructions.

## When someone isn't coming in

In the **Add job to schedule** modal footer, press **Not coming in today**. You'll get a confirmation — and a warning if it will remove existing schedule blocks for that day. Once confirmed, the cell shows a red **Not coming in** chip. Click the chip to undo it.

## Sharing the day's schedule

Press **Share** to open the **Schedule share** modal:

- **Send now** — pick recipients, choose what to include (**Current day**, **Next day**, **Rest of week**), and press **Send now**.
- **Recurring** — set up automatic shares: recipients, days of the week, and a Central-time send time. Existing shares can be paused and resumed under "Active & paused shares".

## Daily rhythm

The Quickfill page embeds this schedule twice — **Schedule** ("Are there any obvious schedule conflicts?") and **Tomorrow's Schedule** ("Who is on what job tomorrow?") — so reviewing dispatch is part of the office's daily loop.
